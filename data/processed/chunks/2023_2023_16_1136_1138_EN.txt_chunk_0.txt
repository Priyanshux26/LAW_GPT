[2023] 16 S.C.R. 1136 : 2023 INSC 1089 CASE DETAILS RAJPAL v. STATE OF RAJASTHAN (Special Leave Petition (Crl.) No.15585 of 2023) DECEMBER 12, 2023. [C. T. RAVIKUMAR AND SANJAY KUMAR, JJ.] HEADNOTES Issue for consideration: Matter pertains to the concern expressed by the Court on the lethargy in following its earlier s in the matter of dealing with bail applications arising out of the same FIR. Judicial notice – Bail applications arising out of the same FIR – Placed before diff erent Judges leading to anomalous situation – Some of the Judges granting bail and some other Judges refusing to grant bail, even when the role attributed to the applicants is almost similar – Earlier s by this Court that it would be appropriate that all the matters pertaining to one FIR are listed before the same Judge so that there is consistency in the s passed – However, the High Courts lethargic in following the earlier s in the matter of dealing with bail applications arising out of the same FIR: Held: In view thereof, concern expressed by this Court on the lethargy in following the earlier s in the matter of dealing with bail applications arising out of the same FIR to avoid confl icting decisions – Issuance of direction to Registrar (Judicial) of the Registry of this Court to communicate this along with a copy of its earlier to the Registrar (Judicial) of all the High Courts. Learned counsel for the petitioner, after arguing for some time, sought permission to withdraw this special leave petition. Recording the said submission, this special leave petition is dismissed as withdrawn, but with the following observation touching the recurrence of a situation occurring despite the repeated s of this Court made with a view to avert such situations. 2. On going through the pleadings in the special leave petition, it is evident that the case of the petitioner is founded on the claim of parity, in view of the factual position that his co-accused was granted bail by a Coordinate Bench of the High Court of Judicature for Rajasthan, Bench at Jaipur but his application for bail was rejected. True that the impugned would reveal that even after going through the dated 29.08.2023 passed in the case of co-accused by the Coordinate Bench, the Bench concerned took exception to it stating that the of this Court in Narcotics Control Bureau v. Mohit Aggarwal, reported in (2022) 6 WLC 58 (SC) was not taken into consideration.
