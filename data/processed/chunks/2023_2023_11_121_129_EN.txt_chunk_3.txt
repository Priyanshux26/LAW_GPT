The guiding principles for determining the market value of the land at the date of the publication of the notifi cation under Section 4(1) in view of Section 23(1) of the said Act, are well settled by this Court in catena of decisions. Accordingly, the determination of market value is the process of predicting an economic event that is assuming a price a willing vendor would off er to a willing purchaser in normal market conditions, but not an event of anxious dealing at armâ€™s length nor a facade of sale nor fi ctitious sale brought about in quick succession or otherwise to infl ate the market value. The size and nature of the lands acquired and size and nature of the lands in respect of which sale instances are produced on record, also would be an important aspects in as much as normally the sale instances of small piece of land can not form reasonable basis to determine the market value of large chunk of land, unless suitable deductions are made in respect of development charges.
