Looking at the overall evidence on record, we find it difficult to come to the conclusion that when the appellant struck the deceased G with the weapon of offence, he intended to cause such bodily injury as was sufficient in the ordinary course of nature to cause death. The weapon of offence in the present case is a common agriculture tool. If a man is hit with a weed axe on the head with sufficient force, it is bound to cause, as here, death.
