I may hasten to add that neither I, or this bench, nor any other court would hold E otherwise to state that influences or even borrowing from other constitutions has not taken place in the formation of our constitution, but, it is equally and ever so more important to note, that the development thereof has been done in specific context of the situations and conditions prevalent in India. 52. In light of the above, I may conclude that placing reliance F therefore, on decisions rendered in a distinct scenario as well as a demonstrably different constitutional position, that too almost singularly, especially in cases which involve considerations of national security and sovereignty, was not justified. 53.
