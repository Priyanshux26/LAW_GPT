Similarly, in the case of P. Venugopal (supra), the Court struck down a legislation which was made singly applicable to the appellant therein being violative of Article 14 of the Constitution. 53. It can thus be seen that wherever this Court has upheld the legislation affecting the single entity, institution or undertaking, it found that it was done in emergent and extreme circumstances preceded by enquiries, parliamentary debates, etc. It was done when the legislature took into consideration the relevant material and found it expedient to do so. 54.
