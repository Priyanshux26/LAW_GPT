The reductio ad absurdum of this doctrine of concurrent jurisdiction came to be felt in a most poignant form in the of Venture Global Engg. v. Satyam Computer Services Ltd., by which this Court held that a foreign award would also be considered as a domestic award and the challenge procedure provided in Section 34 of Part I of the 1996 Act would therefore apply. This led to a situation where the foreign award could be challenged in the country in which it is made; it could also be challenged under Part I of the 1996 Act in India; and could be refused to be recognised and enforced under Section 48 contained in Part II of the 1996 Act.
