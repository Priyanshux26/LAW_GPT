(Criminal Appeal No. 2198 of 2024) 22 April 2024 [Sanjiv Khanna and Dipankar Datta JJ.] Issue for Consideration Whether a Single Judge of the High Court can disagree with an earlier of a Single Judge based on the same set of facts and one trial, and give a conflicting . Headnotes Practice and Procedure – Conflicting Decisions – Two conflicting s by co-ordinate benches arising out of same set of facts and one trial – Subsequent to be treated as referring the matter to larger Bench of two judges for consideration Held: Single Judge of High Court upheld passed by the Special Judge framing charge under Section 302 of the Penal Code, 1860 – attained finality – Subsequently, another Single Judge of High Court disagreed and directed charge under s.302 not to be framed – Same set of facts and one trial leading to two conflicting s – Rule 8 of the Bombay High Court Appellate Side Rules, 1960, considered – If Single Judge is of the opinion that the earlier was unsustainable and contrary to law should have referred to a Division Bench/ two judges Bench – Should not have passed conflicting – Subsequent decision of the Single Judge to be treated as one differing with the view expressed in the earlier and as one referring the matter to a larger bench of two Judges/ Division Bench for consideration – Chief Justice of High Court requested to constitute appropriate Bench.
