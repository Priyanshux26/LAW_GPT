Digital Supreme Court Reports alleged offence committed by the CCL has to be conducted by the Board as a juvenile – Correctness: Held: Section 7 of the Act deals with the procedure in relation to the Board – Sub-Section 3 thereof provides that the Board may act notwithstanding absence of any member of the Board – No passed by the Board shall be invalid by reason only of absence of any member during any stage of proceedings – The proviso thereto provides that at the time of final disposal of the case or making an under Section 18(3) of the Act, there shall be at least two members including the Principal Magistrate – Section 7(4) of the Act provides that in case there is any difference of opinion in the interim or the final disposal, the opinion of the majority shall prevail – Where there is no such majority, the opinion of the Principal Magistrate shall prevail – A perusal of the record shows that after the was reserved on 29.03.2022, the matter was listed on 05.04.2022 for s – The Principal Magistrate recorded his opinion that the CCL is to be tried by the Children’s Court – The other member of the Board recorded his dissent though, no detailed reasons were given as such – In such a situation the opinion of the Principal Magistrate will prevail – In the case in hand the was signed by the Principal Magistrate – Even if the other member of the Board had not signed the and had merely mentioned that he had a dissenting view, without any reasons being recorded, the of the Principal Magistrate will prevail – Thus, passed by the Board as signed by the Principal Magistrate on 05.04.2022 was final.
