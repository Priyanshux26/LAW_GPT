[Paras 48-50] Criminal Law – Investigation – On being interrogated, accused not obligated to confess to the crime: Held: Non-cooperation by the accused is one matter and the accused refusing to confess to the crime is another – There would be no obligation upon the accused that on being interrogated, he must confess to the crime and only thereafter, would the Investigating Officer be satisfied that the accused has cooperated with the investigation.
