DISCUSSION 11. At the outset, it may be noted that the entire case of prosecution hinges on the circumstantial evidence, in as much as there was no eye witness to the incident in question. It is a well settled principle of law that when a case is based on circumstantial evidence, the circumstances proved must point unequivocally to the guilt of the accused and must be incompatible with any theory of his being innocent.
