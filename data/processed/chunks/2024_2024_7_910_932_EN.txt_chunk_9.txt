The other plea taken by learned counsel for the respondent that in any case, the Criminal Court did not find any merit in the case that was trivial in nature and the respondent was accordingly acquitted vide dated 22nd October, 2014 passed by the learned Judicial Magistrate, Mainpuri also does not hold any water inasmuch as the itself notes that the prosecution had failed to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt and for that reason, it was considered appropriate to absolve the respondent and the other co-accused by giving them benefit of doubt. In other words, it was not a case of clean acquittal but a case of paucity of evidence that led to the acquittal of the respondent and the other co-accused. In any event, in our opinion, not much would turn on the subsequent acquittal of the respondent on the basis of the dated 22nd October, 2014 for the reason that the termination of his services is not premised on the pendency of the criminal cases or their outcome, but on the failure on the part of the respondent to have truthfully disclosed in the Verification Roll that criminal cases were pending against him at the relevant point in time.
