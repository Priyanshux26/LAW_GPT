The so-called vendor is not permitted in law to dispossess or to question the title of the C assessee (the so-called vendee). It was for this very practical purpose that the doctrine of the equity of part performance was introduced in the Transfer of Property Act, 1882, by inserting Section 53-A therein. The section specifically allows the doctrine of part performance to be applied to the agreements which, though D required to be registered, are not registered and to transfers not completed in the manner prescribed therefor by any law. The section is, therefore, applicable to cases where the transfer is not completed in a manner required by law unless such a non- compliance with the procedure results in the transfer being void.
