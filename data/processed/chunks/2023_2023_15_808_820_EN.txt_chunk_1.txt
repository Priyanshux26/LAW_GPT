[Para 25] Evidence Act, 1872 – Fact, when said to be ‘disproved’: Held: A fact is said to be “disproved” when, after considering the matters before it, the court either believes that it does not exist or considers its non-existence so probable that a prudent man ought, under the circumstances of the particular case, to act upon the supposition that it does not exist – Fact is said to be “not proved” when it is neither “proved” nor “disproved”.
