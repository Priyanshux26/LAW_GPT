Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 – s. 2 – Civil contempt and criminal Contempt – Distinction: Held: ‘Wilful disobedience’ of a , decree, direction, , writ, or process of a court or wilful breach of an undertaking given to a court amounts to ‘civil contempt’ – Criminal contempt involves ‘scandalising’ or ‘lowering’ the authority of any court; prejudicing or interfering with judicial proceedings; or interfering with or obstructing the administration of justice.
