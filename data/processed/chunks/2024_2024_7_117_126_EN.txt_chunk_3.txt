It is not in dispute that the respondent is dealing with chewing tobacco. From 7th April 2003, the respondent started the practice of packing together 33 pouches of 6 gms each and one pouch of 15 gms of chewing tobacco in a larger poly pack. The Revenue contends that as the larger poly pack has weight and MRP printed on it, the same was a group package intended for retail sale. The case made out in the show cause notices is that the poly pack contains a quantity of more than 10 gms of chewing tobacco, and therefore, exemption under Rule 34(b) of the said Rules will not apply. As can be seen from Clause (b) of Rule 34 of the said Rules, the exemption will apply to any package containing a commodity if the net weight of the commodity is 10 gms or less and if the same is being sold by weight.
