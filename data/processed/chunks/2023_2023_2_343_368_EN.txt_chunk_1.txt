The powers are not pari materia. Bar Council of India has much larger powers and authority. This Court is unable to agree with the reasoning in V. Sudeer that because the State Bar Councilsâ€™ power for providing training or for holding examination was taken away by the 1973 Amendment, it ipso facto C amounts to taking away such powers if they so vested with the Bar Council of India. The legislative object was clear i.e. not to confer such powers on the State Bar Councils. However, that could not affect the position of the power of the Bar Council of India, and naturally such a power existed. If the Bar Council of India never had such a power, then the same could not be read D by implication. But, if the Bar Council of India had sufficient powers, then the 1973 Amendment would not take away those powers of the Bar Council of India as the said amendment did not deal with the aspect of the powers of the Bar Council of India.
