Even otherwise, accepting the stand of the respondents as made in the counter affidavit, there has been a delay of 27/20 days on the part of the Central Government and the Detaining Authority in deciding the representation when it was called from the Prison Authorities after notice was issued in the present matter. We further find that the detention is liable to be quashed and set aside on this ground also. [2024] 9 S.C.R. 353 Jaseela Shaji v. The Union of India & Ors. 72. In the result, we pass the following : (i) The appeal is allowed; (ii) The and of the High Court dated 4th March 2024 in Writ Petition (Criminal) No. 1271 of 2023 is quashed and set aside. (iii) The dated 31st August 2023 passed by the Joint Secretary (COFEPOSA) to the Government of India directing the detention of the detenu is quashed and set aside. (iv) The dated 28th November 2023 passed by the Under Secretary, Government of India confirming the detention of the detenu – Appisseril Kochu Mohammed Shaji (Shaji A.K.) is quashed and set aside.
