[Paras 54, 59] Environment – Protection and preservation – Approach to be adopted by the courts – Constitution of India – Articles 48A, 51A, 21, 14 and 19: Held: This Court has repeatedly reiterated the approach required to be adopted by the courts where the onus is on the violator to prove that there is no environmental degradation – There is a constitutional duty enjoined upon every court to protect and preserve the environment – Courts will have to apply the principle of parens patriae in light of the constitutional mandate enshrined in Articles 48A, 51A, 21, 14 and 19 of the Constitution of India – Therefore, the burden of proof lies on a developer or industrialist and also on the State in a given case to prove that there is no such degradation.
