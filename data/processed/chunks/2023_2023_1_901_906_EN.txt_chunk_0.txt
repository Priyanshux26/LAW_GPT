Feeling aggrieved and dissatisfied with the impugned and passed by the High Court of Delhi at New Delhi in Writ Petition(C) No. 5441 of 2017 by which the High Court has allowed D the said writ petition and has declared that the acquisition proceedings initiated under the Land Acquisition Act, 1894 (hereinafter referred to as “Act, 1894”) with regard to the land in question is deemed to have lapsed under Section 24(2) of the Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, E 2013 (hereinafter referred to as “Act, 2013”), the Delhi Development Authority (DDA) has preferred the present appeal. 2. Having heard the learned counsel appearing on behalf of the respective parties and having gone through the impugned and passed by the High Court, it appears that it was the specific case F on behalf of the LAC/DDA before the High Court and so stated in the counter filed that the possession of the land in question was taken over and handed over to the beneficiary department in the year 2007.
