The cultivation of GM crops in the midst of other indigenous farming would cause unstoppable contamination and cross-pollination. It would also adversely affect non-target organisms like soil microbes, insects, bees, butterflies, birds or even mammals which are critical to the agro-ecosystem. l. There is a glaring lack of indigenous study on the impact of GM crops on human health. It is particularly worrying because certain studies on animals revealed complications including infertility, immune problems, accelerated aging, insulin regulation, and changes in major organs.
