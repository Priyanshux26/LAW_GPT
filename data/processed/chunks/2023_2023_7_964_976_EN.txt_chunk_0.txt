XXI, rr. 92, 94, 95 – Limitation Act, 1963 – Art. 134 – What is the starting point of limitation for filing an application under Rule 95 of XXI of C the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 – Held: On a conjoint reading of sub-rule (1) of r. 92 and r. 94 of XXI of CPC, it is apparent that the of confirmation of sale under sub-rule (1) of Rule 92 of XXI culminates into a grant of a sale certificate under Rule 94 of XXI – The date of sale to be incorporated in the D sale certificate is the date of passing of the of sale confirmation – The very fact that Rule 94 of XXI incorporates a requirement of issuing a sale certificate shows that the Legislature was of the view that mere of confirmation of auction may not be sufficient – The certificate is ultimately the evidence of the fact that the auction in favour of the person to whom a certificate is issued, has been E confirmed by the Executing Court – Prima facie, the only way of avoiding inconsistency between Rule 95 of XXI of CPC and Article 134 of the Limitation Act is to read into Article 134 that the starting point for making an application under Rule 95 of XXI of CPC is the date on which a certificate recording confirmation F of auction sale is actually issued to the purchaser – The decision of the Co-ordinate Bench in the case of Pattam Khader Khan requires reconsideration by the larger Bench – In view of the Court, the larger Bench will have to decide the issue relating to the starting point of limitation for making an application under Rule 95 of XXI of CPC.
