The controversy in the present appeals centers around land in A Zamin Survey Nos. 5105 and 5108 in Salem Zameen Estate which corresponds to O.T.S. Nos. 2253 and 2210 respectively. The appellant herein is Salem Muslim Burial Ground Protection Committee, Salem1. The State of Tamil Nadu (Revenue Department) is respondent No.1, and respondent Nos.2 and 3 are Commercial Taxes C and Religious Endowments and the Director of Survey and Settlement Office respectively, who are formal parties. Respondent Nos.4 to 23 are the claimants, who alleged that they are residing over the “suit land” and are the settlers thereon from times immemorial having acquired rights over it through their predecessors-in-interest. The old records reveal D that the “suit land” at one point of time was used as a burial ground paramboke but the municipality ed its closure for health reasons somewhere in the year 1867 and an alternative site was allotted for use as a burial ground. 5.
