Both, the pari passu principle and the anti-deprivation principle sprout from the common ground that parties cannot contract out of an insolvency legislation. Their distinction lies in their impacts. The pari passu principle is aimed at ensuring that all creditors get their proportional dues by preventing any one creditor from getting more than their deserved share.39 The anti-deprivation principle on the other hand aims at conservation of the insolvent estate for the benefit of the creditors.40 26.
