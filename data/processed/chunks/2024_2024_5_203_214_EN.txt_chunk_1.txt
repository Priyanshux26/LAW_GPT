[Para 13] Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 – ss.147, 138 – Offences to be compoundable: Held: As per s.147, all offences punishable under the Negotiable Instruments Act are compoundable – However, unlike s.320 of CrPC, the NI Act does not elaborate upon the manner in which offences should be compounded – In cases of s.138, the accused must try for compounding at the initial stages instead of the later stage, however, there is no bar to seek the compounding of the offence at later stages of criminal proceedings including after conviction, like the present case. All the same, for some reasons, M/s Farmax failed to procure and supply this machine to respondent No.2-complainant. Thereafter, the appellant issued 5 cheques to the complainant towards return of the advance money. Admittedly, some of these cheques were dishonoured and in Nov-Dec 2013 the complainant initiated proceedings under section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act (hereinafter referred to as “NI Act”).
