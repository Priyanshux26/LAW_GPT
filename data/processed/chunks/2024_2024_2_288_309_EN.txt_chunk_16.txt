The circumstances in this case are far from conclusive and a conclusion of guilt could not be drawn from them. To sustain a conviction, the Court must form the view that the accused “must have” committed the offence, and not “may have”. As noted in Sharad Birdichand Sarda11, the distinction between “may have” and “must have” is a legal distinction and not merely a grammatical one. 39.
