The Advocates Act was enacted to implement the recommendations of the All-India Bar Committee. According to the Statement of Objects and Reasons, the main features of the enactment are: (i) establishment of an All-India Bar Council and a common roll of advocates, and advocates on the common roll having a right to practice in any part of the country and any Court, including the Supreme Court; (ii) integration of the bar into a single class of legal practitioners known as advocates; (iii) prescription of a uniform qualification for the admission of persons to be advocates; (iv) division of advocates into senior advocates and other advocates based on merit; and (v) creation of autonomous Bar Councils, one for the whole of India and one for each State.
