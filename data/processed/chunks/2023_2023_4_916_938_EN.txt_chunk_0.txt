No. 55 of 2023) B MAY 04, 2023 [KRISHNA MURARI AND SANJAY KAROL, JJ.] Customs Act, 1962 – ss.123, 127B – Seized goods specified u/s.123, remedy of settlement u/s.127B if available – Held: Per Krishna Murari, J. In cases of seizure within the customs area, s.123 cannot apply and the accused is entitled to the remedy of settlement – C Decision passed by the Bombay High Court in Suresh Raheja case states the correct position of law – Per Sanjay Karol, J. Proviso to s.127B(1) specifies certain categories of goods that are barred from the jurisdiction of the settlement commission including goods mentioned u/s.123 and goods relating to NDPS Act – Thus, s.127B D would not apply for settlement in respect of the goods enumerated u/s.123 – In view of divergence of opinion, matter to be placed before the Chief Justice of India for appropriate s – Constitution of India – Article 21.
