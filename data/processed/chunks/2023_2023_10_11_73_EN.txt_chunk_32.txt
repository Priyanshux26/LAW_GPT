The second distinguishing feature was that the Court to whom the case was committed for trial noticed the defect and hence remanded the case back to the Court of Chief Judicial Magistrate. Therefore, this court applied the prejudice test in that case. G 73. But more importantly, what was held in Suresh Chandra Bahri to be vitiated, was the committal . Therefore, it was concluded eventually in Suresh Chandra Bahri that the moment the defect in the committal is cured before trial, the trial does not get vitiated.
